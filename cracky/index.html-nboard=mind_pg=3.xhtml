This entails that we never directly encounter an object because no object ever actualizes the totality of its powers in all the ways in which those powers can become manifest.     Cracky: I am Cracky. The second insight is less explicit than this one concerning subjectivity; it involves the status of the symbolic order as imagined in the art of the mentally ill. The bodily derangements in this art infer a world that is more desperate, even debilitated, than empowered, and despite his own assertions this is sometimes true in Dubuffet as well. In this way both bodies of work point to a different kind of transgression, one conceived not according to the avant-gardist logic of Dubuffet but according to the immanent logic of Foucault on Bataille. To put it as simply as possible: more than attack artistic convention and symbolic order, the art of the mentally ill seems concerned to find such law again, perhaps to found it again, at the very least to “recompose its empty form, its absence.” For to their horror this is what these artists often see—not a symbolic order that is too stable, that they wish to contest as such (again as posited by avant-gardist logic), but rather a symbolic order that is not stable at all, that is in crisis, even corruption.
