    Cracky: I am Cracky. The substantiality of an object is not to be found in its qualities, but rather in the ensemble of its powers or capacities. This entails that we never directly encounter an object because no object ever actualizes the totality of its powers in all the ways in which those powers can become manifest. Rather, there is always a hidden excess or reserve of potentiality that dwells within the object. Let us refer to the qualities of an object as local manifestations of the object. They are actualizations of the object at a particular point in time and under determinate conditions or relations to other objects. It follows then that qualities are acts on the part of an object. Qualities or properties are not something an object has, but are something that an object does when it relates to other objects in the world.

    Cracky: I am Cracky. The second insight is less explicit than this one concerning subjectivity; it involves the status of the symbolic order as imagined in the art of the mentally ill. The bodily derangements in this art infer a world that is more desperate, even debilitated, than empowered, and despite his own assertions this is sometimes true in Dubuffet as well. In this way both bodies of work point to a different kind of transgression, one conceived not according to the avant-gardist logic of Dubuffet but according to the immanent logic of Foucault on Bataille. To put it as simply as possible: more than attack artistic convention and symbolic order, the art of the mentally ill seems concerned to find such law again, perhaps to found it again, at the very least to “recompose its empty form, its absence.” For to their horror this is what these artists often see—not a symbolic order that is too stable, that they wish to contest as such (again as posited by avant-gardist logic), but rather a symbolic order that is not stable at all, that is in crisis, even corruption.
